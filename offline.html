Why Offline

Care documentation that works with no signal.

Every app in the DSP Suite runs entirely on the device. No internet, no accounts, no cloud. The basement med room with one bar of service is exactly where it's meant to work.

Get Started → See all apps ↓

Built offline from day one — not patched for it later

Most documentation software assumes a steady connection. Group homes don't have one. Staff work in basements, older buildings, and rural homes where signal drops and wifi is an afterthought. When the tool needs the cloud and the cloud isn't there, documentation simply doesn't happen — and the details fade before the shift ends.

The DSP Suite takes the opposite approach. The entire suite never connects to any server. Logging an incident, recording a med pass, capturing a goal note, dictating with voice — all of it happens on the device, instantly, whether or not there's a connection.

What "offline" actually means here

  • No network calls, ever. The apps make zero requests to any server. There is no backend to be down.
  • On-device voice transcription. Speak your notes hands-free. Audio is processed locally and never sent to Google, Apple, or any third party.
  • Instant, every time. Nothing waits on a round-trip. The right screen is one tap away even with the phone in airplane mode.
  • You choose when anything leaves. Records stay on the device until you deliberately export a PDF or file to your own email or sharing app.

This isn't a setting you switch on. It's how the apps are built — which is also why the privacy story is so clean.
