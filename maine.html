Made for the way Maine residential care actually runs

Maine DSPs supporting adults with intellectual and developmental disabilities work across home support and residential settings — often in older or rural homes where connectivity is unreliable and there's no IT staff to lean on. The DSP Suite is built for exactly that: it installs on an Android phone, needs no network, and staff are using it on day one.

It supports documentation for the kinds of programs delivered under MaineCare Sections 18, 20, 21, and 29 — incident logging, medication administration records, goal progress, resident check-ins, and per-resident mileage and spending. It does not replace your system of record or claim to satisfy any specific regulation on its own; it's the on-shift capture layer that gets accurate details down before they fade, so what lands in your formal documentation is complete.

Why local matters here

  • No cloud dependency in homes where wifi is an afterthought.
  • Anonymous identifiers only (R1–R10, H1–H10) — nothing identifying leaves the device.
  • Direct support from the developer — a DSP working in Maine, not an out-of-state vendor queue.
